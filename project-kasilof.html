Project Overview

Development, livestock, and foot traffic are threatening parts of this world-class salmon fishery. See how Grizzly Outdoors Corps is contributing to efforts to help restore 750 feet of streambank and vital fish habitat.

Field Notes

From Repurposed Rootwads to Renewed Habitats

Even a small stretch of bank erosion can affect the migration and spawning habits of millions of freshwater fish. Project Kasilof is using native trees and vegatation to both stabilize banks and provide additional habitat.

Step 1

Repurposed tree roots add stabilization.

Step 2

Followed by layers of brush, soil, and vegetation.

Step 3

Over time, banks stabilize and plants return – giving salmon vital cover, as well as feeding and rest areas.
